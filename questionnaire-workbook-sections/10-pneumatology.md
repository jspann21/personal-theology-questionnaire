# Pneumatology

### Q46. Does Spirit baptism normally coincide with conversion and incorporation into Christ?
- Category: Pneumatology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes the relation of conversion, incorporation into Christ, and Spirit baptism.
- Yes: `Spirit baptism at conversion`

### Q47. Is speaking in tongues the initial physical evidence of Spirit baptism?
- Category: Pneumatology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Yes: `Tongues as the initial physical evidence`

### Q48. Are miraculous sign gifts intended for the whole church age?
- Category: Pneumatology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Yes: `Continuationism of sign gifts`

### Q49. Should congregational prophecy be understood as potentially fallible and therefore to be weighed?
- Category: Pneumatology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects how prophecy is understood in the congregation and whether it must be publicly tested.
- Yes: `Fallible congregational prophecy to be weighed`
- Yes proof texts: Acts 21:4, 10-11; 1 Corinthians 14:29-32; 1 Thessalonians 5:20-21
- No: `Prophecy as infallible revelatory utterance`
- No proof texts: Deuteronomy 18:20-22; Jeremiah 23:28-29; 2 Peter 1:21
- Note: The issue is whether ordinary congregational prophecy can be mixed and therefore tested, not whether Scripture can err.

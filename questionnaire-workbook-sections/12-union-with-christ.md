# Union With Christ

### Q80. Is union with Christ primarily covenantal and representative rather than primarily mystical and participatory?
- Category: Union with Christ
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes whether union is framed mostly in legal-covenantal or transformative-participatory terms.
- Yes: `Representative-covenantal emphasis`
- Yes proof texts: Romans 5:12-21; 1 Corinthians 15:22; Ephesians 1:3-14
- No: `Participatory-mystical emphasis`
- No proof texts: John 15:1-7; Galatians 2:20; 2 Peter 1:4
- Note: The issue is one of organizing emphasis. Most evangelical traditions affirm both representative and participatory elements in union with Christ.

### Q81. Does sacramental participation ordinarily serve as a means of deepening believers' communion with Christ?
- Category: Union with Christ
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects sacramental theology and the means of grace.
- Yes: `Stronger means-of-grace sacramentalism`
- Yes proof texts: John 6:53-58; 1 Corinthians 10:16-17
- No: `Communion with Christ not ordinarily mediated sacramentally`
- No proof texts: Luke 23:43; Romans 14:17; Hebrews 10:19-25
- Note: This overlaps with `Q94-Q95`, but here the issue is believers' communion with Christ, not the mechanics of the ordinances themselves.

### Q82. Should "in Christ" language control Christian identity more than individualized decision language?
- Category: Union with Christ
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects conversion language, discipleship, and theological anthropology.
- Yes: `Union-centered identity`
- Yes proof texts: Ephesians 1:3-14; 2 Corinthians 5:17; Galatians 2:20
- No: `Decision-centered conversion language priority`
- No proof texts: John 1:12; Romans 10:9-10; Acts 16:31
- Note: This is about theological and pastoral emphasis, not about whether personal faith and conscious response to the gospel are necessary.

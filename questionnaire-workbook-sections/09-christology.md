# Christology

### Q38. Is Jesus Christ one divine person in two distinct natures, fully God and fully man forever?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is the basic christological grammar behind the incarnation, atonement, mediation, and worship of Christ.
- Yes: `Chalcedonian two-natures christology`
- Yes proof texts: John 1:1, 14; Philippians 2:6-8; Colossians 2:9; Hebrews 2:14-17
- No: `Kenotic, reductionist, or non-Chalcedonian alternatives`
- No proof texts: John 14:28; Mark 13:32; Luke 2:52
- Note: This asks about Christ's person and natures, not about whether he truly suffered, learned, or was tempted according to his humanity.

### Q39. Was Jesus conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is a creed-level doctrine tied to the incarnation, Christ's identity, and basic Christian orthodoxy.
- Yes: `Virgin birth affirmed`
- Yes proof texts: Isaiah 7:14; Matthew 1:18-25; Luke 1:34-35
- No: `Natural-conception or non-literal infancy narrative reading`
- No proof texts: Matthew 13:55; Mark 6:3; Galatians 4:4
- Note: The `No` position is usually driven more by historical-critical skepticism or non-literal readings than by any explicit biblical denial.

### Q40. Was Christ unable to sin?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects the impeccability debate and how one relates Christ's person to the reality of his temptations.
- Yes: `Impeccability`
- Yes proof texts: John 8:29; 2 Corinthians 5:21; Hebrews 7:26
- No: `Peccability in the sense of real possibility`
- No proof texts: Hebrews 2:17-18; Hebrews 4:15; Matthew 4:1-11
- Note: This asks whether Christ could have sinned according to his person, not whether his temptations were real.

### Q41. Did Christ descend to the dead between crucifixion and resurrection?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This bears on the descensus clause of the creed and how Christ's death is interpreted between crucifixion and resurrection.
- Yes: `Descended to the dead`
- Yes proof texts: Acts 2:27, 31; Ephesians 4:9; 1 Peter 3:18-20
- No: `No distinct descent beyond death, burial, or immediate paradise`
- No proof texts: Luke 23:43, 46; John 19:30
- Note: The question includes descensus formulations such as Hades or the dead, not merely Christ's burial.

### Q42. Did Jesus rise bodily from the dead on the third day?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is a primary gospel doctrine and one of the most basic points on which a church would expect pastoral clarity and confessional fidelity.
- Yes: `Bodily resurrection of Christ`
- Yes proof texts: Luke 24:39-43; John 20:27; Acts 2:24-32; 1 Corinthians 15:3-8, 12-20
- No: `Spiritualized or non-bodily resurrection reading`
- No proof texts: Matthew 28:13; Luke 24:11; 1 Corinthians 15:44
- Note: The `No` position usually rests on skepticism or a redefinition of resurrection rather than on a straightforward biblical counter-case, but it is too foundational to leave unstated.

### Q43. Is the atonement best explained primarily as penal substitution?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects how the cross is centrally interpreted among the various biblical atonement motifs.
- Yes: `Penal substitution as the primary explanation`
- Yes proof texts: Isaiah 53:4-6, 10; Mark 10:45; Romans 3:25-26; 2 Corinthians 5:21; 1 Peter 2:24
- No: `Multiple-motif or alternative primary explanation`
- No proof texts: Colossians 2:15; Hebrews 2:14-15; 1 Peter 2:21; 1 John 3:8
- Note: This does not deny other motifs like victory, example, or reconciliation; it asks what is primary.

### Q44. Did Christ's active obedience contribute positively to believers' justification?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes justification, imputation, and what believers receive positively in union with Christ.
- Yes: `Active obedience imputed in justification`
- Yes proof texts: Romans 5:18-19; Galatians 4:4-5; Matthew 3:15; Hebrews 10:5-10
- No: `Justification grounded chiefly in Christ's atoning death`
- No proof texts: Romans 3:24-26; Romans 5:9; 2 Corinthians 5:21; 1 Peter 2:24

### Q45. Is Christ's present heavenly session an active mediatorial reign over the church and the nations now?
- Category: Christology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects kingdom theology and whether Christ's present heavenly session is understood as a presently active mediatorial reign.
- Yes: `Present mediatorial reign now`
- Yes proof texts: Psalm 110:1-4; Matthew 28:18; Ephesians 1:20-23; Hebrews 7:25
- No: `Session affirmed but reign treated chiefly as future or earthly`
- No proof texts: Luke 19:11-12; 1 Corinthians 15:24-28; Revelation 20:1-6

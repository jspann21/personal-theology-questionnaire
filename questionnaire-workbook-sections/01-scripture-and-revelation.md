# Scripture And Revelation

### Q1. Is Scripture alone the final infallible norm for doctrine and practice?

- Category: Scripture and revelation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This determines the final court of appeal when Scripture, tradition, reason, and experience appear to compete.

- Yes: `Sola Scriptura / final infallible norm`

- Yes proof texts: Isaiah 8:20; Mark 7:6-13; Acts 17:11; 2 Timothy 3:16-17

- No: `Prima Scriptura / Scripture with binding apostolic-traditional authority`

- No proof texts: 2 Thessalonians 2:15; 1 Timothy 3:15; Hebrews 13:7

### Q2. Is Scripture, in its original writings, wholly true and without error in all that it affirms?

- Category: Scripture and revelation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This clarifies whether biblical authority includes full truthfulness in all the text's affirmations or only infallibility in limited doctrinal or salvific matters.

- Yes: `Full inerrancy`

- Yes proof texts: Psalm 12:6; Psalm 19:7; John 10:35; John 17:17; 2 Timothy 3:16

- No: `Limited inerrancy / infallibility in saving purpose`

- No proof texts: Joshua 10:13; Matthew 13:32; John 20:30-31

### Q3. Has special revelation of a canonical, foundational sort ceased with the apostolic witness?

- Category: Scripture and revelation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes how one understands prophecy, revelation, doctrinal authority, and the sufficiency of the canon.

- Yes: `Closed canon / foundational revelation complete`

- Yes proof texts: Hebrews 1:1-2; Jude 3; Ephesians 2:20; Revelation 22:18-19

- No: `Continuing revelatory gifts / non-canonical revelation continues`

- No proof texts: Acts 2:17-18; 1 Corinthians 14:1, 29-31; 1 Thessalonians 5:20-21

- Note: This question is about foundational or revelatory speech, not ordinary illumination, providential guidance, or wise counsel.

### Q4. Is Scripture sufficiently clear in its central saving message to be understood by ordinary believers?

- Category: Scripture and revelation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects confidence in lay Bible reading, discipleship, and the role of church authority in interpretation.

- Yes: `Perspicuity in essentials`

- Yes proof texts: Psalm 19:7; Psalm 119:130; Luke 24:27, 45; 2 Timothy 3:15

- No: `Material obscurity / stronger interpretive dependence on church teachers`

- No proof texts: 2 Peter 3:16; Acts 8:30-31; Nehemiah 8:8

### Q5. Should doctrine normally be built first through grammatical-historical exegesis rather than allegorical or spiritualized readings?

- Category: Scripture and revelation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This establishes the basic interpretive posture by which doctrinal conclusions are formed.

- Yes: `Grammatical-historical priority`

- Yes proof texts: Nehemiah 8:8; Luke 24:27; Acts 17:2-3; 2 Timothy 2:15

- No: `Typological-allegorical priority in some loci`

- No proof texts: Galatians 4:24-26; 1 Corinthians 10:1-11; Hebrews 7:1-10

- Note: The issue is not whether Scripture contains types, but whether doctrine should ordinarily be built from the literal sense before typological or spiritual extension.

### Q6. Should explicit New Testament interpretation norm disputed Old Testament theological trajectories?

- Category: Scripture and revelation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This bears directly on covenant theology, Israel-church questions, law, promise, and prophecy.

- Yes: `Progressive revelation / NT priority`

- Yes proof texts: Luke 24:44-47; Acts 2:16-36; Hebrews 8:6-13; 2 Corinthians 1:20

- No: `Literal-grammatical continuity / OT promises retain plain force unless explicitly revised`

- No proof texts: Genesis 17:7-8; Jeremiah 31:35-37; Romans 11:28-29

- Note: This is not a question of whether the New Testament is authoritative, but how it fulfills, expands, or redirects prior revelation.

### Q7. Is natural revelation sufficient to render humanity accountable but insufficient to save without the gospel?

- Category: Scripture and revelation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes exclusivism, inclusivism, missionary urgency, and the doctrine of revelation.

- Yes: `General revelation condemns, special revelation saves`

- Yes proof texts: Psalm 19:1-4; Romans 1:18-21; Romans 10:13-17

- No: `Broader revelational inclusivism`

- No proof texts: John 1:9; Acts 10:34-35; Romans 2:14-16

- Note: This concerns saving sufficiency, not whether general revelation communicates real truth about God or whether providence may prepare someone to hear the gospel.

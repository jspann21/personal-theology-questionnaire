# Anthropology

### Q30. Is humanity best described dichotomously rather than trichotomously?
- Category: Anthropology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes theological anthropology, sanctification models, and the body-soul-spirit relationship.
- Yes: `Dichotomy`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 2:7; Matthew 10:28; 1 Corinthians 5:3; Revelation 6:9
- No: `Trichotomy`
- No proof texts: 1 Thessalonians 5:23; Hebrews 4:12

### Q31. Does the image of God remain in fallen humanity after sin?
- Category: Anthropology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects ethics, human dignity, and how fallenness is described.
- Yes: `Image retained though marred`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 9:6; James 3:9
- No: `Image largely lost and restored chiefly in redemption`
- No proof texts: Ephesians 4:24; Colossians 3:10; Romans 3:10-18

### Q32. Are men and women equal in dignity yet assigned distinct covenantal roles in home and church?
- Category: Anthropology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This frames the complementarian-egalitarian debate.
- Yes: `Complementarianism`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 2:18; 1 Corinthians 11:3; Ephesians 5:22-33; 1 Timothy 2:12-13
- No: `Egalitarianism`
- No proof texts: Galatians 3:28; Acts 2:17-18; Romans 16:1, 7; 1 Corinthians 11:5
- Note: The dispute is over differentiated roles and offices, not over equal worth, shared image-bearing dignity, or spiritual standing before God.

### Q33. Is the conscience a reliable moral guide apart from scriptural calibration?
- Category: Anthropology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects moral reasoning, counseling, and the relation of conscience to revelation.
- Yes: `Stronger natural-law confidence in conscience`
- Yes proof texts: Romans 2:14-15; Acts 23:1; 1 Timothy 1:5
- No: `Conscience requires constant biblical calibration`
- No proof texts: Jeremiah 17:9; 1 Corinthians 4:4; Titus 1:15
- Note: This does not ask whether conscience is real or useful, but whether it can function dependably without continuing correction by Scripture.

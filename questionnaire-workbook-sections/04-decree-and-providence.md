# Decree And Providence

### Q16. Does God ordain whatsoever comes to pass?
- Category: Decree and providence
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is one of the major dividing lines between strong divine determinism and broader synergistic or libertarian models.
- Yes: `Comprehensive divine decree`
- Yes proof texts: Isaiah 46:10; Ephesians 1:11; Acts 2:23; Acts 4:27-28
- No: `God permits rather than ordains many events`
- No proof texts: Deuteronomy 30:19; Luke 7:30; 1 Corinthians 10:13
- Note: This is the broadest decree question in the section. `Q19-Q20` then narrow the discussion to providence over ordinary events and evil in particular.

### Q17. Is libertarian freedom necessary for moral responsibility?
- Category: Decree and providence
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes how human accountability and divine sovereignty fit together.
- Yes: `Libertarian freedom`
- Yes proof texts: Deuteronomy 30:19; Joshua 24:15; Matthew 23:37
- No: `Compatibilism`
- No proof texts: Genesis 50:20; Acts 2:23; Philippians 2:12-13

### Q18. Does God will in more than one sense, such as decretive will and preceptive will?
- Category: Decree and providence
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects the interpretation of texts about divine desire, command, decree, and human disobedience.
- Yes: `Two-wills or multi-aspect willing in God`
- Yes proof texts: Deuteronomy 29:29; Matthew 23:37; 1 Timothy 2:4; Acts 2:23
- No: `Single-aspect willing / caution against internal distinctions`
- No proof texts: Numbers 23:19; Psalm 115:3; Isaiah 46:10

### Q19. Is meticulous providence the best description of God's governance over ordinary events?
- Category: Decree and providence
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects prayer, trust, secondary causes, and the theology of ordinary life.
- Yes: `Meticulous providence`
- Yes proof texts: Proverbs 16:33; Proverbs 21:1; Matthew 10:29-30; Ephesians 1:11
- No: `Broader providence with greater creaturely contingency`
- No proof texts: Jeremiah 19:5; Luke 13:34; 1 Corinthians 10:13

### Q20. Does God's providence include ordaining even evil events for wise and good ends, while God himself remains unstained by evil?
- Category: Decree and providence
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is central to theodicy, providence, and the moral language used of divine sovereignty.
- Yes: `Comprehensive providence over evil acts`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 50:20; Isaiah 45:7; Acts 2:23; Acts 4:27-28
- No: `Permission model / God is never the intending agent of evil`
- No proof texts: James 1:13; 1 John 1:5; Habakkuk 1:13
- Note: The question is not whether God commits evil, but whether he positively ordains evil events through secondary causes for wise ends or merely permits them.

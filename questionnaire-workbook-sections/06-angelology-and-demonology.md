# Angelology And Demonology

### Q27. Can a true believer be subject to severe inward demonic influence beyond mere external oppression?
- Category: Angelology and demonology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes pastoral care, the language of possession or demonization, and the perceived limits of demonic influence on believers.
- Yes: `Severe inward demonic influence possible in believers`
- Yes proof texts: Luke 13:16; Ephesians 4:27; 2 Corinthians 12:7
- No: `Indwelling-possession incompatible with union with Christ`
- No proof texts: 1 Corinthians 6:19; 2 Corinthians 6:14-16; 1 John 4:4
- Note: The terminology often turns on distinctions between possession, demonization, oppression, and inward influence.

### Q28. Is territorial or strategic-level spiritual warfare a valid category for ministry strategy?
- Category: Angelology and demonology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects prayer strategy, missions practice, and whether territorial spirits should shape ministry planning.
- Yes: `Territorial or strategic-level warfare model`
- Yes proof texts: Daniel 10:13, 20; Ephesians 6:12; Revelation 12:7-9
- No: `Ordinary resistance without territorial-spirit strategy`
- No proof texts: James 4:7; 1 Peter 5:8-9; Luke 10:17-20
- Note: This asks whether Scripture warrants strategic practices built around territorial powers, not whether cosmic evil powers are real.

### Q29. Should Christians directly address demonic powers in ordinary ministry?
- Category: Angelology and demonology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes deliverance practice and how Christians ordinarily confront demonic powers in ministry.
- Yes: `Direct address or rebuke of demonic powers`
- Yes proof texts: Luke 9:1; Luke 10:17; Acts 16:18
- No: `Ordinary resistance through prayer to God rather than direct address`
- No proof texts: Zechariah 3:2; Jude 8-10; 2 Peter 2:10-11
- Note: This concerns ordinary ministry practice, not whether Christ or the apostles ever directly rebuked demons.

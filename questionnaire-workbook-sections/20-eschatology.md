# Eschatology

### Q126. Will Christ return before a future earthly millennium?
- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This is the defining question between premillennial and non-premillennial systems.

- Yes: `Premillennialism`
- Yes proof texts: Revelation 19:11-20:6; Zechariah 14:4-9; Matthew 19:28
- No: `Amillennialism or postmillennialism`
- No proof texts: John 5:28-29; 1 Corinthians 15:23-28; Revelation 20 read symbolically
- Note: This asks about the sequence of Christ's return and the millennium; `Q127` then asks more specifically how Revelation 20 should be read.

### Q127. Should Revelation 20's thousand years be read literally as a future earthly reign?

- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This clarifies how apocalyptic genre is handled in millennial doctrine.

- Yes: `Literal millennium`

- Yes proof texts: Revelation 20:1-6; Isaiah 11:1-10; Isaiah 65:20-25

- No: `Symbolic millennium`

- No proof texts: John 5:28-29; 1 Corinthians 15:23-28; Revelation 20:1-6

- Note: The key issue is whether Revelation 20 recapitulates the present church age or describes a distinct future earthly kingdom after Christ's return.

### Q128. Should the millennium be understood as a period of widespread gospel success and discipling of the nations before Christ returns?

- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This distinguishes postmillennial expectation from amillennial and premillennial readings that do not expect a broadly Christianized world before the return of Christ.

- Yes: `Postmillennial expectation`

- Yes proof texts: Psalm 2:8; Isaiah 2:2-4; Matthew 13:31-33; Matthew 28:18-20

- No: `Amillennial or premillennial expectation`

- No proof texts: Matthew 24:9-14; 2 Timothy 3:1-5, 12-13; 2 Peter 3:3-4

### Q129. Will the church be raptured before a future tribulation?
- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects tribulation theology, imminence, and the relation between Israel and the church.

- Yes: `Pretribulation rapture`
- Yes proof texts: 1 Thessalonians 1:10; 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17; 1 Thessalonians 5:9; Revelation 3:10
- No: `Posttribulation or single-return view`
- No proof texts: Matthew 24:29-31; John 17:15; 2 Thessalonians 2:1-3
- Note: This is distinct from `Q131`. One may affirm a future tribulation while still denying a pretribulational rapture.

### Q130. Does Scripture teach a distinct future conversion or restoration of ethnic Israel?

- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This clarifies Romans 11 and the future of ethnic Israel in redemptive history.

- Yes: `Future restoration of ethnic Israel`

- Yes proof texts: Romans 11:25-27; Zechariah 12:10; Ezekiel 37:21-28

- No: `Present inclusion in Christ fulfills the promise`

- No proof texts: Galatians 3:28-29; Ephesians 2:14-16; 2 Corinthians 1:20

### Q131. Is the tribulation principally future rather than fulfilled primarily in AD 70 and the church age?

- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects how the Olivet Discourse and Revelation are read.

- Yes: `Futurist tribulation`

- Yes proof texts: Daniel 9:27; Matthew 24:21; Revelation 6-19

- No: `Preterist or partial-preterist emphasis`

### Q132. Will there be a final conscious eternal punishment of the wicked?
- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects the doctrine of final judgment and the nature of hell.

- Yes: `Eternal conscious punishment`
- Yes proof texts: Matthew 25:46; Mark 9:43-48; Revelation 14:11; Revelation 20:10, 15
- No: `Conditional immortality / annihilationism`
- No proof texts: Matthew 10:28; John 3:16; 2 Thessalonians 1:9
- Note: The dispute is over the nature and duration of final punishment, not whether the wicked face final judgment.

### Q133. Is the intermediate state conscious for believers before the resurrection?

- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes funeral theology, hope, and the doctrine of the soul.

- Yes: `Conscious intermediate state`

- Yes proof texts: Luke 23:43; Philippians 1:23; 2 Corinthians 5:8; Revelation 6:9-11

- No: `Soul sleep`

- No proof texts: Ecclesiastes 9:5; John 11:11-14; 1 Thessalonians 4:13-15

### Q134. Will the dead be raised bodily for final judgment and everlasting life or punishment?

- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This is a basic creedal doctrine that shapes Christian hope, final judgment, and the continuity between redemption and embodied human life.

- Yes: `Final bodily resurrection of the dead`

- Yes proof texts: Daniel 12:2; John 5:28-29; Acts 24:15; 1 Corinthians 15:42-44, 52

- No: `Spiritualized or non-bodily final-state model`

- No proof texts: Ecclesiastes 9:5; 1 Corinthians 15:50; 2 Corinthians 5:1-8

- Note: The `No` position usually depends on spiritualizing resurrection language or collapsing final hope into the intermediate state rather than on any straightforward biblical denial of bodily resurrection.

### Q135. Should the new creation be understood as cosmic renewal rather than total replacement?
- Category: Eschatology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes resurrection theology, ecological theology, and the continuity of God's world.

- Yes: `Renewal or restoration model`
- Yes proof texts: Romans 8:19-23; 2 Peter 3:13; Revelation 21:1-5
- No: `Total dissolution and replacement model`
- No proof texts: 2 Peter 3:10-12; Revelation 21:1
- Note: The issue is continuity versus discontinuity between the present creation and the new creation, not whether the final state is radically transformed.

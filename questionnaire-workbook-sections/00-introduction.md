# Personal Systematic Theology Questionnaire Workbook

This workbook is designed to help a Christian articulate a personal, printed, verse-supported statement of belief in the major loci of evangelical systematic theology. It is intentionally broad, comparative, and rigorous. It includes both widely shared convictions and major intramural debates.

It is organized around 20 major doctrinal loci and 135 yes/no prompts.

Use it slowly. The goal is not speed but clarity.

## Primary Goal

The immediate goal is not to force a person into a label, but to help that person answer enough tightly framed doctrinal questions to produce a serious personal statement of theology:

- in first-person form

- with explicit biblical support

- with clear awareness of live evangelical alternatives

- with enough precision to be printed and handed to another believer as a faithful summary of present convictions

## How To Use This Workbook

1. Work through the questions in order.

2. Mark `Yes` or `No` on each item.

3. If you are uncertain, still mark the answer you presently find more persuasive and record the uncertainty in a note.

4. Record any additional qualifications, distinctions, or narrower definitions in the margin or beneath the question.

5. Where a question reflects a real evangelical debate, review the proof texts listed for both answers before deciding.

6. Read those proof texts in their immediate and canonical context rather than using the references as isolated slogans.

7. Use your completed answers with the companion personal theology build guide to draft a fuller personal theology document.

## Standards For Careful Use

- Distinguish explicit biblical statements from broader theological inferences.

- Pay attention to genre, redemptive-historical setting, and authorial context.

- Note when an answer depends on one disputed text versus a wider canonical pattern.

- Use the proof texts as starting points for judgment, not as substitutes for exegesis.

- Where the debate is real, aim to understand the strongest version of both positions before concluding.

## Conventions

- `Category` identifies the doctrinal locus.

- `Why this matters` explains why the question belongs in a systematic theology statement.

- `Yes` and `No` list the general position commonly associated with each answer.

- `Proof texts` are references only, so the workbook remains usable across Bible translations and printable at length.

- `Note` appears only where the dispute usually turns on a key interpretive distinction.

# Theology Proper

### Q8. Does God possess exhaustive foreknowledge of future free human acts?
- Category: Theology proper

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects providence, prophecy, prayer, human freedom, and the nature of divine knowledge.

- Yes: `Exhaustive definite foreknowledge`
- Yes proof texts: Isaiah 46:9-10; Psalm 139:4, 16; Matthew 26:34; Acts 2:23
- No: `Open theist or dynamic omniscience model`
- No proof texts: Genesis 22:12; Jeremiah 3:7; Jonah 3:10
- Note: This question concerns the scope of divine knowledge; the providence section later asks how that knowledge relates to decree, will, and human responsibility.

### Q9. Is God immutable in his being and covenant faithfulness, even though Scripture describes him relationally?

- Category: Theology proper

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This determines how anthropomorphic passages are handled and how divine consistency is defined.

- Yes: `Classical immutability`

- Yes proof texts: Malachi 3:6; James 1:17; Hebrews 13:8; Numbers 23:19

- No: `Relational mutability emphasis`

- No proof texts: Genesis 6:6; Exodus 32:14; Jonah 3:10

### Q10. Does God love all people salvifically in some real sense, not only the elect?
- Category: Theology proper

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This bears on common grace, the free offer of the gospel, and the character of divine love.

- Yes: `Universal salvific love / common grace emphasis`
- Yes proof texts: Ezekiel 18:23, 32; Matthew 5:44-45; John 3:16; 1 Timothy 2:4
- No: `Particular redemptive love emphasis`
- No proof texts: Deuteronomy 7:7-8; John 10:11, 26; Ephesians 5:25
- Note: The disagreement is not over whether God is kind and patient toward all in providence, but whether Scripture teaches a redemptive or salvific love directed universally.

### Q11. Should God's glory be treated as the highest end of all his works?

- Category: Theology proper

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This governs theological method, ethics, worship, and the way divine purpose is summarized.

- Yes: `God's glory as ultimate end`

- Yes proof texts: Isaiah 43:6-7; Romans 11:36; Ephesians 1:5-12; 1 Corinthians 10:31

- No: `Love or relational communion treated as the primary summary`

- No proof texts: Micah 6:8; Matthew 22:37-40; John 3:16; 1 John 4:8-10

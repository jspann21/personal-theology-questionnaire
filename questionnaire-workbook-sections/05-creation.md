# Creation

### Q21. Should Genesis 1 be read as teaching six ordinary days of creation?
- Category: Creation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects the doctrine of creation, the fall, death, and how biblical cosmology is interpreted.

- Yes: `Young-earth / literal-day reading`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 1:5, 8, 13, 19, 23, 31; Exodus 20:11; Exodus 31:17
- No: `Analogical day / framework / old-earth compatible reading`
- No proof texts: Genesis 2:4; Psalm 90:4; 2 Peter 3:8
- Note: This is narrower than `Q22`. A reader may reject ordinary-day duration while still reading the chapter as orderly and historically referential.

### Q22. Do the "days" of Genesis 1 require a sequential chronological reading?

- Category: Creation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This further clarifies whether Genesis 1 functions mainly as chronology, literary structure, or both.

- Yes: `Sequential chronology emphasis`

- Yes proof texts: Genesis 1:3-31; Exodus 20:11

- No: `Literary framework or analogical sequence`

- No proof texts: Genesis 2:4; Psalm 104:1-30

### Q23. Must Adam be understood as a historical individual rather than only an archetypal symbol?

- Category: Creation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This bears directly on original sin, federal headship, and Paul's Adam-Christ parallel.

- Yes: `Historical Adam`

- Yes proof texts: Genesis 2:7; Luke 3:38; Romans 5:12-19; 1 Corinthians 15:21-22, 45

- No: `Archetypal Adam emphasis`

- No proof texts: Genesis 1:26-27; Genesis 2-3 read typologically; Hosea 6:7

### Q24. Should human origins be understood as involving the special creation of Adam and Eve rather than common ancestry with other living creatures?

- Category: Creation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This addresses one of the clearest present-day evangelical divides over creation, human uniqueness, and the relation of Scripture to evolutionary models.

- Yes: `Special creation of the first humans`

- Yes proof texts: Genesis 2:7, 21-22; Matthew 19:4-6; Romans 5:12; 1 Corinthians 15:45-47

- No: `Evolutionary creation / common ancestry model`

- No proof texts: Genesis 1:27; Psalm 104:24-30; Acts 17:26

### Q25. Was death in the created order absent before human sin?
- Category: Creation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects views of the fall, animal death, and the scope of Adamic curse.

- Yes: `No death before the fall`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 1:29-30; Romans 5:12; Romans 8:20-22
- No: `Human death through sin, but not necessarily all creaturely death`
- No proof texts: Psalm 104:21, 29; Job 38:39-41; Romans 5:12
- Note: The question is whether all creaturely death entered through Adam's fall, not merely whether human death is tied to sin.

### Q26. Is the image of God located chiefly in humanity's representative function rather than in substantive qualities?

- Category: Creation

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes anthropology, ethics, dignity, and the doctrine of humanity after the fall.

- Yes: `Functional-representative image`

- Yes proof texts: Genesis 1:26-28; Psalm 8:5-8

- No: `Substantive or relational image emphasis`

- No proof texts: Genesis 9:6; James 3:9; Ephesians 4:24; Colossians 3:10

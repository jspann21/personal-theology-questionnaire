# Mission And Evangelism

### Q116. Should the church's mission be defined primarily as disciple-making rather than broad cultural transformation?
- Category: Mission and evangelism
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes whether the church's mission is defined chiefly by the Great Commission or by a broader cultural mandate.
- Yes: `Disciple-making as the church's primary mission`
- Yes proof texts: Matthew 28:18-20; Luke 24:46-49; Acts 1:8
- No: `Broader culture-renewal or transformation emphasis`
- No proof texts: Luke 4:18-19; Matthew 5:13-16; Matthew 28:18-20
- Note: This does not deny that Christians should do public good; it asks what the institutional church is primarily commissioned to do.

### Q117. Is a public invitation or altar call a biblically warranted evangelistic practice?
- Category: Mission and evangelism
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects evangelistic method and the structure of gospel appeals.
- Yes: `Invitationist practice`
- Yes proof texts: Acts 2:37-41; Acts 8:36-38; Acts 16:30-33
- No: `Ordinary means and catechetical caution`
- No proof texts: Luke 14:25-33; Acts 20:20-21; Romans 10:17
- Note: This is a question of evangelistic method, not whether hearers should be urgently called to repent and believe.

### Q118. Should missionary strategy prioritize unreached peoples over already-evangelized contexts?
- Category: Mission and evangelism
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes strategy, funding, and definitions of missionary stewardship.
- Yes: `Unreached-priority strategy`
- Yes proof texts: Romans 15:20-21; Matthew 24:14; Revelation 7:9
- No: `Local-faithfulness parity`
- No proof texts: Acts 1:8; Acts 13:1-3; 2 Timothy 4:2
- Note: The issue is strategic priority, not whether the church has obligations both near and far.

### Q119. Is apologetics a necessary ordinary component of evangelism?
- Category: Mission and evangelism
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects training, preaching, and how the church commends the faith publicly.
- Yes: `Apologetic integration`
- Yes proof texts: 1 Peter 3:15; Acts 17:2-3, 17; Philippians 1:7
- No: `Kerygmatic priority over apologetic method`
- No proof texts: 1 Corinthians 1:17-25; 1 Corinthians 2:1-5
- Note: This asks about ordinary evangelistic shape, not whether Christians should ever answer objections or defend the faith.

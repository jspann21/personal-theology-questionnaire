# Israel, Church, And Covenants

### Q120. Is the church the continuation or fulfillment of God's one covenant people rather than a distinct parenthetical people?
- Category: Israel, church, and covenants

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This is one of the defining divides between covenantal and dispensational systems.

- Yes: `Covenant theology / fulfillment model`
- Yes proof texts: Galatians 3:7, 29; Ephesians 2:11-22; 1 Peter 2:9-10
- No: `Dispensational distinction`
- No proof texts: Romans 11:25-29; 1 Corinthians 10:32; Revelation 7:4-8; Revelation 21:12-14
- Note: This overlaps with `Q122`, but `Q120` asks about the identity of God's people across redemptive history, while `Q122` asks more specifically about prophetic interpretation.

### Q121. Do Old Testament land promises retain a future national fulfillment for ethnic Israel?

- Category: Israel, church, and covenants

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects prophetic interpretation, political theology, and biblical-theological continuity.

- Yes: `Future national-land fulfillment`

- Yes proof texts: Genesis 15:18-21; Jeremiah 31:35-37; Ezekiel 36:24-28; Romans 11:28-29

- No: `Land fulfilled in Christ and new creation`

- No proof texts: Matthew 5:5; Romans 4:13; Ephesians 2:11-22; Hebrews 11:13-16

### Q122. Should Israel and the church be kept distinct in prophetic interpretation?
- Category: Israel, church, and covenants

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This influences nearly every major eschatological framework.

- Yes: `Israel-church distinction`
- Yes proof texts: Daniel 9:24; Romans 11:25-29; 1 Corinthians 10:32
- No: `Israel-church unity in Christ`
- No proof texts: Galatians 6:16; Ephesians 2:14-16; 1 Peter 2:9-10
- Note: This is narrower than `Q120`. One may affirm strong continuity in the people of God while still debating how prophecy should be mapped onto Israel and the church.

### Q123. Is the new covenant made only with regenerate members?
- Category: Israel, church, and covenants

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes baptism, church membership, and covenant theology.

- Yes: `Regenerate new-covenant membership`
- Yes proof texts: Jeremiah 31:31-34; Hebrews 8:8-12; Hebrews 10:14-18
- No: `Visible covenant administration includes children`
- No proof texts: Genesis 17:7; Isaiah 59:21; Acts 2:39; 1 Corinthians 7:14
- Note: The issue is the covenant's membership principle, not whether local churches can perfectly identify the regenerate in practice.

### Q124. Should the Abrahamic covenant be treated as one promise fulfilled in Christ rather than as separate redemptive tracks?

- Category: Israel, church, and covenants

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This helps determine how promise, people, and inheritance are related across the canon.

- Yes: `Christocentric single-promise fulfillment`

- Yes proof texts: Galatians 3:16-29; Romans 4:11-17

- No: `Multiple-track promise structure`

- No proof texts: Genesis 15:18-21; 2 Samuel 7:12-16; Jeremiah 33:14-26

### Q125. Is the Mosaic law as covenant obsolete under the new covenant?
- Category: Israel, church, and covenants

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects Christian ethics, sabbath theology, and the use of the Old Testament law.

- Yes: `Mosaic covenant obsolete`
- Yes proof texts: Romans 6:14; Romans 7:4-6; 2 Corinthians 3:6-11; Hebrews 8:13
- No: `Stronger continuity of Mosaic covenant obligations`
- No proof texts: Matthew 5:17-19; Romans 3:31
- Note: This asks whether the Mosaic covenant remains binding as covenant, not whether Old Testament law still instructs Christians or reveals God's moral character.

# Hamartiology

### Q34. Did Adam's first sin bring guilt to all humanity by imputation?
- Category: Hamartiology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes original sin, federal headship, and the grounds on which humanity stands condemned in Adam.
- Yes: `Imputed Adamic guilt`
- Yes proof texts: Romans 5:12-19; 1 Corinthians 15:21-22; Psalm 51:5
- No: `Inherited corruption without immediate imputed guilt`
- No proof texts: Deuteronomy 24:16; Ezekiel 18:20; Romans 2:6
- Note: This asks about guilt in Adam, not merely whether Adam's fall corrupted all his descendants.

### Q35. Is original sin best understood as total inability apart from grace?
- Category: Hamartiology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects anthropology, grace, and whether fallen humans can come to God apart from prior divine enabling.
- Yes: `Total inability apart from grace`
- Yes proof texts: John 6:44; Romans 8:7-8; 1 Corinthians 2:14; Ephesians 2:1-3
- No: `Original sin without moral inability`
- No proof texts: Deuteronomy 30:11-14; Acts 17:27; Romans 2:14-15

### Q36. Does sin primarily involve disordered love and idolatry rather than only rule-breaking?
- Category: Hamartiology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects whether sin is diagnosed chiefly at the level of the heart's loves and worship or primarily as transgression of law.
- Yes: `Idolatry or disordered love as the primary description`
- Yes proof texts: Exodus 20:3-5; Ezekiel 14:3; Romans 1:21-25
- No: `Lawbreaking as the primary description`
- No proof texts: 1 John 3:4; James 2:10; Romans 3:23
- Note: This is a question of primary theological description, not a denial that sin is both inward and outward.

### Q37. Are all sins equal in demerit before God?
- Category: Hamartiology
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This shapes moral theology, punishment, and whether Scripture warrants strong distinctions between greater and lesser sins.
- Yes: `Equal demerit in principle before God`
- Yes proof texts: James 2:10; Galatians 3:10; Romans 6:23
- No: `Degrees of gravity and guilt among sins`
- No proof texts: John 19:11; Ezekiel 8:6, 13, 15; 1 John 5:16-17
- Note: This asks about demerit before God, not whether all sins have identical historical or social consequences.

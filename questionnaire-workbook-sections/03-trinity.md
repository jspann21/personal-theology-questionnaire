# Trinity

### Q12. Should the eternal relation between Father and Son be described in terms of eternal authority and submission?
- Category: Trinity
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects how one relates the eternal Trinity to order, role language, and claims about equality within the Godhead.
- Yes: `Eternal relations of authority and submission`
- Yes proof texts: John 5:19; John 6:38; 1 Corinthians 11:3; 1 Corinthians 15:28
- No: `Eternal generation without authority-submission ordering`
- No proof texts: John 1:1; John 5:18; John 10:30; Philippians 2:6
- Note: This concerns the eternal relation of Father and Son, not merely the incarnate Son's voluntary obedience in the economy of redemption.

### Q13. Should the Son's relation to the Father be described as eternal generation rather than as a merely temporal or incarnational sonship?
- Category: Trinity
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is a central Nicene claim and helps distinguish classical trinitarian grammar from more recent evangelical alternatives.
- Yes: `Eternal generation affirmed`
- Yes proof texts: John 1:14, 18; John 5:26; Hebrews 1:3-5
- No: `Sonship treated chiefly as messianic, incarnational, or non-eternal relation`
- No proof texts: Luke 1:35; Acts 13:33; Hebrews 1:5 read temporally
- Note: This asks how the Son's personal distinction from the Father should be described eternally, not whether the Son is fully divine.

### Q14. Does the Old Testament contain genuine, though veiled, indications of plurality within the Godhead?
- Category: Trinity
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This bears on continuity between the Testaments and whether trinitarian doctrine is only retrospective or genuinely adumbrated in the Old Testament.
- Yes: `Veiled plurality within the Godhead in the OT`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 1:26; Psalm 110:1; Isaiah 48:16
- No: `Strict monotheistic singularity without real intra-divine plurality disclosed`
- No proof texts: Deuteronomy 6:4; Isaiah 44:6; Isaiah 45:5
- Note: The issue is not whether the Old Testament teaches the Trinity with New Testament clarity, but whether it contains real anticipatory indications.

### Q15. Should the filioque be affirmed, meaning that the Spirit proceeds from the Father and the Son?
- Category: Trinity
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects classical trinitarian dogmatics and how one describes the Spirit's eternal procession.
- Yes: `Filioque affirmed`
- Yes proof texts: John 15:26; John 16:7; John 20:22; Galatians 4:6
- No: `Procession from the Father alone`
- No proof texts: John 15:26; John 14:26
- Note: This concerns eternal procession, not merely the Son's temporal sending of the Spirit in redemptive history.

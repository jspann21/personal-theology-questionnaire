# Christian Ethics

### Q107. Is the Mosaic civil law directly binding on modern states unless specifically repealed?
- Category: Christian ethics
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This clarifies theonomy, civil law continuity, and Christian political theology.
- Yes: `Theonomic continuity`
- Yes proof texts: Matthew 5:17-19; Isaiah 2:3-4; 2 Timothy 3:16-17
- No: `General-equity or new-covenant ethic`
- No proof texts: Acts 15:19-20; Romans 13:8-10; Galatians 3:23-25
- Note: This asks whether Mosaic civil law binds modern polities as law, not whether Old Testament civil texts still instruct Christians morally and politically.

### Q108. Is capital punishment for murder a continuing moral norm rooted before Moses?

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

### Q109. Is just-war participation compatible with Christian discipleship?
- Category: Christian ethics
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is one of the oldest debates in Christian ethics.
- Yes: `Just-war or lawful-force ethic`
- Yes proof texts: Luke 3:14; Romans 13:4; Acts 10:1-2
- No: `Christian pacifism`
- No proof texts: Matthew 5:38-48; Matthew 26:52; Romans 12:17-21
- Note: Answering `Yes` does not imply that all wars are just, only that military participation can in principle be compatible with Christian obedience.

### Q111. Should abortion be treated as the taking of human life from conception?
- Category: Christian ethics
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This affects bioethics, human dignity, and the start of personhood.
- Yes: `Pro-life from conception`
- Yes proof texts: Psalm 139:13-16; Jeremiah 1:5; Luke 1:41-44; Exodus 21:22-25

### Q112. Do biblical prohibitions of same-sex intercourse apply universally, rather than only to exploitative, idolatrous, or culturally bound contexts?
- Category: Christian ethics
- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No
- Why this matters: This is one of the most pressing moral debates in contemporary Christian theology.
- Yes: `Historic Christian sexual ethic`
- Yes proof texts: Genesis 2:24; Leviticus 18:22; Romans 1:26-27; 1 Corinthians 6:9-11; 1 Timothy 1:10
- No: `Affirming revisionist reading`
- No proof texts: Acts 10:15; Galatians 3:28; Romans 13:8-10
- Note: The `No` case typically depends on re-reading the disputed prohibition texts through arguments about exploitation, purity categories, covenantal inclusion, and the relation of creation order to new-covenant ethics.

# Ecclesiology

### Q83. Is the local church properly constituted only by professing believers?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This is a key Baptist versus paedobaptist covenant-community question.

- Yes: `Regenerate church membership`

- Yes proof texts: Acts 2:41-42; Acts 8:12; 1 Corinthians 1:2

- No: `Mixed covenant community`

- No proof texts: Genesis 17:7; Acts 2:39; 1 Corinthians 7:14

- Note: The dispute is about the church's ordinary membership principle, not about whether hypocrites or false professors may still be present in practice. This question also overlaps with the baptism question in `Q91`, but it is narrower: here the issue is who should belong to the covenant community as such.

### Q84. Is congregational polity preferable to presbyterial or episcopal polity?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes governance, authority, and accountability structures.

- Yes: `Congregationalism`

- Yes proof texts: Matthew 18:17-20; Acts 6:3-6; 1 Corinthians 5:4-5; 2 Corinthians 2:6

- No: `Presbyterial or episcopal polity`

- No proof texts: Acts 15:1-29; Acts 20:17, 28; Titus 1:5

- Note: This question concerns where final governing authority ordinarily resides in the church. It is not identical to `Q85`, which asks whether a plurality of elders is the normative leadership pattern.

### Q85. Should a plurality of elders ordinarily lead each local congregation?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This clarifies whether shared elder leadership is normative or merely one possible pattern.

- Yes: `Plurality of elders`

- Yes proof texts: Acts 14:23; Acts 20:17; Philippians 1:1; Titus 1:5; James 5:14

- No: `Single-bishop or primary-pastor model acceptable as norm`

- No proof texts: 1 Timothy 3:1-2; 1 Timothy 5:17; Revelation 2:1

- Note: One may affirm elder plurality while still answering `Yes` or `No` to `Q84`; the two questions separate leadership pattern from the location of final authority.

### Q86. Are women biblically permitted to serve as pastors or elders?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This is one of the major contemporary debates in ecclesial theology.

- Yes: `Egalitarian eldership`

- Yes proof texts: Galatians 3:28; Acts 2:17-18; Romans 16:1, 7; 1 Corinthians 11:5

- No: `Male eldership / complementarian polity`

- No proof texts: 1 Timothy 2:12; 1 Timothy 3:1-2; Titus 1:5-6; 1 Corinthians 14:34-35

- Note: The dispute turns not only on isolated texts, but on whether restrictions tied to teaching and eldership are transcultural or local and situational. This question is about the governing/teaching office, not about whether women may exercise many other significant ministries in the church.

### Q87. Are women biblically permitted to serve as deacons?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This is a real church-defining secondary issue in many evangelical settings and is often treated differently from the pastor-elder question.

- Yes: `Women deacons permitted`

- Yes proof texts: Romans 16:1; 1 Timothy 3:11

- No: `Male-only diaconate`

- No proof texts: Acts 6:1-6; 1 Timothy 3:12

- Note: This question is intentionally narrower than `Q86`. Many complementarians distinguish sharply between the office of elder and the office or function of deacon.

### Q88. Should church discipline include formal exclusion from the Lord's Table and membership in serious unrepentant sin?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes holiness, pastoral authority, and the visible boundaries of the church.

- Yes: `Formal church discipline`

- Yes proof texts: Matthew 18:15-17; 1 Corinthians 5:1-13; 2 Thessalonians 3:6, 14-15

- No: `Informal restorative discipline without formal exclusion emphasis`

- No proof texts: Galatians 6:1; Matthew 13:28-30; Luke 9:54-56

- Note: The issue here is whether discipline can culminate in a formal ecclesial act of exclusion, not whether patient restoration and private correction are essential pastoral duties. It also presupposes some answer to `Q90`, since formal exclusion is clearest where membership is more formally defined.

### Q89. Is the visible church broader than any one denomination and inclusive of all true believers?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This determines how catholicity, unity, and denominational identity are understood.

- Yes: `Broader visible catholicity`

- Yes proof texts: John 10:16; John 17:20-23; Ephesians 4:4-6

- No: `Stronger denominational or institutional identity`

- No proof texts: 1 Timothy 3:15; Revelation 2-3; Acts 2:42

- Note: This question is about the breadth of the church across communions, not about whether local congregations must maintain doctrinal and disciplinary boundaries. Many who answer `Yes` still hold a strong doctrine of local church order and confessional identity.

### Q90. Are formal membership covenants biblically warranted?

- Category: Ecclesiology

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This affects pastoral oversight and how accountability is structured.

- Yes: `Formal membership and accountability`

- Yes proof texts: Acts 2:41-47; 1 Corinthians 5:12-13; Hebrews 13:17

- No: `Informal fellowship model`

- No proof texts: Matthew 18:20; Romans 16:5; Acts 8:3

- Note: This question asks whether formalized membership structures are biblically warranted as a prudent ecclesial expression. It does not deny that Christians must gather, submit to leaders, and practice mutual accountability even if they answer `No`.

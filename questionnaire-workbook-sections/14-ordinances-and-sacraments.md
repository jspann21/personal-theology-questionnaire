# Ordinances And Sacraments

### Q91. Should baptism be administered only to those who personally profess faith?
- Category: Ordinances and sacraments

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This is the defining divide between credobaptist and paedobaptist practice.

- Yes: `Credobaptism`
- Yes proof texts: Matthew 28:19-20; Acts 2:41; Acts 8:12, 36-38; Acts 18:8
- No: `Paedobaptism`
- No proof texts: Genesis 17:7-12; Acts 2:39; Acts 16:15, 33; Colossians 2:11-12
- Note: This overlaps with `Q83` and `Q123`, but here the question is the proper subjects of baptism rather than the broader constitution of the covenant community.

### Q92. Is immersion the normative apostolic mode of baptism?
- Category: Ordinances and sacraments

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes baptismal practice and symbolism.

- Yes: `Immersion normativity`
- Yes proof texts: Mark 1:10; John 3:23; Acts 8:38-39; Romans 6:3-4
- No: `Affusion or sprinkling acceptable`
- No proof texts: Ezekiel 36:25; Acts 16:33; Hebrews 10:22
- Note: This asks about mode, not about the proper recipients or efficacy of baptism.

### Q93. Does baptism ordinarily function as a sign and seal rather than as the moment of regeneration itself?
- Category: Ordinances and sacraments

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This clarifies sacramental efficacy and the relation of water baptism to salvation.

- Yes: `Sign-and-seal or symbolic efficacy view`
- Yes proof texts: Luke 23:43; Acts 10:44-48; Romans 4:11; 1 Corinthians 1:17; Ephesians 2:8-9
- No: `Baptismal regeneration or stronger sacramental efficacy`
- No proof texts: John 3:5; Acts 2:38; Acts 22:16; 1 Peter 3:21
- Note: The issue is not whether baptism is important or commanded, but whether saving grace is ordinarily tied to the sacramental act itself.

### Q94. Is the Lord's Supper primarily memorial and proclamatory rather than a means of real spiritual feeding?

- Category: Ordinances and sacraments

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This differentiates memorial, Reformed, and stronger sacramental views.

- Yes: `Memorialism`

- Yes proof texts: Luke 22:19; 1 Corinthians 11:24-26

- No: `Real spiritual presence or stronger sacramental feeding`

- No proof texts: John 6:53-58; 1 Corinthians 10:16-17

- Note: Some evangelical traditions deny transubstantiation yet still answer `No` here because they affirm a real spiritual participation in Christ at the Table.

### Q95. Should the Lord's Supper be restricted to baptized believers under church oversight?
- Category: Ordinances and sacraments

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This shapes fencing the table and ecclesial boundaries.

- Yes: `Restricted or close communion`
- Yes proof texts: Matthew 28:19-20; Acts 2:41-42; 1 Corinthians 10:16-17; 1 Corinthians 11:27-29
- No: `Open communion`
- No proof texts: Luke 14:21-23; Romans 14:1; Romans 15:7
- Note: This concerns fencing the Table, not the question in `Q94` of what sort of presence or participation occurs in the Supper itself.

### Q96. Is foot washing an abiding church ordinance?
- Category: Ordinances and sacraments

- Answer: [ ] Yes  [ ] No

- Why this matters: This addresses how direct Jesus' commands in John 13 are for church practice.

- Yes: `Foot washing as ordinance`
- Yes proof texts: John 13:14-15; 1 Timothy 5:10
- No: `Foot washing as paradigm of humble service`
- No proof texts: Luke 7:44; John 13 read symbolically
- Note: The issue is whether Jesus instituted a continuing church rite, not whether humble service to others is obligatory.

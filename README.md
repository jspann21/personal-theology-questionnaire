# Systematic Theology Workbook

An interactive workbook for clarifying your doctrinal convictions and turning them into a personal, verse-supported theology statement.

Systematic Theology Workbook is built for Christians who want to do more than identify with a tradition or hold a loose collection of theological opinions. It provides a structured way to think through the major loci of doctrine, record where you are persuaded, note where you are uncertain, and produce a draft that reads like a coherent statement of belief.

## What It Is

This project combines a theological questionnaire with a writing tool.

At its core is a guided workbook covering 20 major areas of systematic theology through 135 doctrinal prompts. Each prompt is meant to force clarity. Instead of leaving convictions vague, it asks the user to make concrete judgments, compare competing readings, and identify where stronger study is still needed.

The app then uses those answers to generate a personal theology draft that can be revised into a more polished final statement.

## What Makes It Useful

- It helps organize beliefs that are often held only implicitly.
- It exposes tensions between doctrines that might otherwise stay hidden.
- It forces specific choices instead of broad labels.
- It keeps Scripture close to the process by attaching proof texts to both sides of each question.
- It turns raw answers into a draft document you can actually use.

## How It Works

The workbook leads the user through a sequence of tightly framed yes/no doctrinal questions.

Each question includes:

- the doctrinal category it belongs to
- a short explanation of why the issue matters
- the general position associated with each answer
- proof texts for both sides
- room for personal notes and qualifications

After the questionnaire is complete, the answers are compiled into a first-person theology draft. That draft can be generated either as readable prose or as a tighter outline, then revised into a clearer final statement.

## What It Covers

The workbook spans the major categories of evangelical systematic theology, including:

- Scripture and revelation
- Theology proper
- Trinity
- Decree and providence
- Creation
- Anthropology
- Hamartiology
- Christology
- Pneumatology
- Soteriology
- Union with Christ
- Ecclesiology
- Ordinances and sacraments
- Spiritual gifts
- Worship and ministry
- Christian ethics
- Mission and evangelism
- Israel, church, and covenants
- Eschatology

## Who It Is For

This project is for anyone who wants to articulate their theology more carefully:

- Christians trying to understand the overall shape of their beliefs
- teachers, students, or pastors who want a structured doctrinal self-audit
- readers working through differences between major evangelical systems
- anyone trying to produce a personal theology document that is clearer than a set of disconnected notes

## What You End Up With

By the end of the process, the goal is not merely a completed questionnaire. The goal is a documentable theology.

You should come away with:

- a clearer map of your convictions
- a better sense of where your views are settled or provisional
- a more coherent understanding of how your doctrines fit together
- a draft statement of belief that can be refined, printed, or shared

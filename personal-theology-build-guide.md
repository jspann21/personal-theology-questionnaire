# Personal Theology Build Guide

This guide shows how to turn the completed questionnaire workbook into a personal, printed statement of belief. The goal is a document that reads like a coherent confession rather than a stack of disconnected answers.

The questionnaire workbook is intentionally front-loaded with yes/no decisions so that the final document can be stronger, cleaner, and more synthetic. The workbook gives you doctrinal raw material; this guide helps you turn that material into a readable personal systematic theology.

## Purpose

Use this guide to produce a document that says, in your own words:

- what you believe

- why you believe it

- where you ground it in Scripture

- where you hold your conclusions more tentatively

Your final theology document does not need to imitate any one denomination's confession. It should be a clear, organized, verse-supported statement of your present convictions.

## Before You Draft

Before writing your final statement, perform this audit on the completed workbook:

1. Mark the answers that most strongly define your overall theological identity.

2. Mark the answers you hold more tentatively.

3. Revisit any answers that create tension with each other across loci.

4. Reduce repeated ideas so one conviction is not restated in five different sections without need.

5. Identify which proof texts are primary and which are only secondary supports.

The workbook contains 135 prompts, but your final document should not feel like 135 miniature essays. It should read like one integrated theological account.

## How To Build Each Section

For each doctrinal section, follow the same workflow:

1. Review all questionnaire answers in that category.

2. Identify the questions that most define your position.

3. Collapse overlapping answers into 2-5 paragraph-length affirmations.

4. Add verse references that most directly support your conclusion.

5. If your answer is qualified, say so plainly.

Where a question is heavily disputed, include the conclusion you reached and let the final sentence briefly show why you rejected the main alternative.

Use this sentence pattern repeatedly:

`I believe that ... because ...`

Optional expansions:

- `I distinguish this from ...`

- `I understand the main alternative to be ...`

- `I hold this with greater / lesser confidence because ...`

- `The principal texts that ground this conclusion are ...`

## Writing Rules For The Final Document

- Write in the first person: `I believe`, `I affirm`, `I deny`, `I hold`.

- Prefer positive affirmations before denials.

- Keep proof texts in parentheses or short end-of-sentence lists.

- Use 2-6 references per paragraph, not every reference from the workbook.

- When a doctrine is disputed, state your view and briefly name the alternative without caricature.

- If you are undecided, say so explicitly.

Suggested confidence labels:

- `Firm conviction`

- `Provisional conclusion`

- `Open question requiring further study`

## Section-By-Section Build Instructions

### 1. Scripture and Revelation

Use questions `Q1-Q6` to state:

- your doctrine of biblical authority

- your view of canon and ongoing revelation

- your doctrine of clarity and sufficiency

- your basic hermeneutical posture

- your view of general and special revelation

Template:

`I believe Scripture is / is not the final infallible norm for doctrine and practice. I believe the canon is / is not closed in a foundational sense. I approach interpretation primarily through ... The main texts guiding these conclusions are ...`

### 2. Theology Proper

Use questions `Q7-Q10` to state:

- your doctrine of divine foreknowledge

- your view of immutability

- your account of divine love

- your summary of God's ultimate end

Template:

`I believe God does / does not possess exhaustive foreknowledge of future free acts. I understand God's immutability to mean ... I summarize God's ultimate purpose as ...`

### 3. Trinity

Use questions `Q11-Q13` to state:

- how you describe eternal relations in the Godhead

- whether you see trinitarian plurality in the Old Testament

- whether you affirm the filioque

Template:

`I believe the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are one God in three persons. I describe the relation of Father and Son as ... I affirm / do not affirm the filioque because ...`

### 4. Decree and Providence

Use questions `Q14-Q18` to state:

- your doctrine of decree

- your view of freedom and responsibility

- how you explain God's willing and permission

- how you speak about evil under providence

Template:

`I believe God does / does not ordain whatsoever comes to pass. I understand human freedom as ... I explain evil under providence by saying ...`

### 5. Creation

Use questions `Q19-Q23` to state:

- your reading of Genesis 1

- your doctrine of Adam

- your view of death before the fall

- your doctrine of the image of God

Template:

`I read Genesis 1 as ... I believe Adam was / was not a historical individual in the full sense. I understand the image of God primarily as ...`

### 6. Angelology and Demonology

Use questions `Q24-Q26` to state:

- what demons can and cannot do to believers

- how spiritual warfare should be practiced

- whether direct rebuke or deliverance language belongs in normal ministry

Template:

`I believe demonic opposition to believers should be understood as ... I practice spiritual warfare primarily through ...`

### 7. Anthropology

Use questions `Q27-Q30` to state:

- your body-soul-spirit anthropology

- your doctrine of the image after the fall

- your doctrine of sex, role, and conscience

Template:

`I understand the human person as ... I believe the divine image after the fall is ... I hold that men and women are ...`

### 8. Hamartiology

Use questions `Q31-Q34` to state:

- your view of original sin

- whether Adamic guilt is imputed

- how sin should be described

- whether there are degrees of sin

Template:

`I believe humanity inherits ... from Adam. I define sin primarily as ... and I hold that sins are / are not equal in demerit because ...`

### 9. Christology

Use questions `Q35-Q39` to state:

- whether you affirm impeccability

- how you understand the descent clause

- what model of the atonement you give primacy

- whether active obedience is imputed

- how you describe Christ's present reign

Template:

`I believe Jesus Christ is fully God and fully man. I hold that his temptations mean ... I understand the atonement primarily as ... I believe Christ now reigns ...`

### 10. Pneumatology

Use questions `Q40-Q45` to state:

- your doctrine of Spirit baptism

- your view of tongues

- whether gifts continue

- your view of prophecy

- how sanctification normally works

Template:

`I believe baptism in the Spirit occurs ... I believe tongues are ... I affirm / deny the continuation of miraculous gifts. I understand prophecy in the church as ...`

### 11. Soteriology

Use questions `Q46-Q70` to write the longest section in the final document.

Break it into these subheadings:

1. Human inability and prevenient grace

2. Election and foreknowledge

3. Extent and intent of the atonement

4. Calling, regeneration, faith, and repentance

5. Justification and righteousness

6. Union, adoption, and assurance

7. Sanctification and perseverance

8. The order of salvation

9. Divine sovereignty and human responsibility

10. The necessity of conscious faith in Christ

Suggested synthesis logic:

- `Q46-Q47` tell you whether your system is monergistic, Arminian/Wesleyan, or some hybrid.

- `Q48-Q49` define your doctrine of election.

- `Q50-Q51` define your doctrine of atonement extent and intent.

- `Q52-Q56` define how grace reaches the sinner and how conversion occurs.

- `Q57-Q58` define your doctrine of justification.

- `Q59-Q60` define how you organize assurance and salvation benefits.

- `Q61-Q64` define perseverance, apostasy, and sanctification.

- `Q65-Q68` help tie initiation, sanctification, and ordo salutis together.

- `Q69-Q70` summarize the macro-framework of your whole soteriology.

Template:

`I believe fallen humanity is / is not morally unable to come to Christ apart from prior grace. I believe God does / does not grant prevenient grace to all persons. I believe election is ... I believe Christ died ... I believe regeneration occurs ... I believe justification is ... I believe believers will / may not persevere ...`

When writing this section, do not merely list answers. Convert the answers into an internally coherent account.

### 12. Union With Christ

Use questions `Q71-Q73` to state:

- whether union is mainly representative, participatory, or both

- whether sacramental practice ordinarily deepens communion with Christ

- whether identity language should center on union

Template:

`I understand union with Christ primarily as ... I believe this union is expressed and nourished through ...`

### 13. Ecclesiology

Use questions `Q74-Q80` to state:

- what a church is

- who belongs to it

- how it is governed

- who may lead

- how discipline and catholicity are understood

Template:

`I believe a local church should be composed of ... I believe its ordinary form of government is ... I believe the pastorate / eldership should be ... I believe church discipline should ...`

### 14. Ordinances and Sacraments

Use questions `Q81-Q86` to state:

- who should be baptized

- how baptism should be administered

- what baptism does

- what the Lord's Supper is

- who should come to the Table

Template:

`I believe baptism should be administered to ... by ... I believe baptism is ... I believe the Lord's Supper is primarily ... and should be received by ...`

### 15. Spiritual Gifts

Use questions `Q87-Q91` to state:

- your view of tongues, prophecy, healing, deliverance, and miraculous gifts

- whether sign gifts remain ordinary or unusual

Template:

`I believe spiritual gifts are / are not distributed in the same way today as in the apostolic era. I believe prophecy should be treated as ... I believe healing ministry should be practiced ...`

### 16. Worship and Ministry

Use questions `Q92-Q96` to state:

- your philosophy of worship

- your view of preaching

- your sabbath or Lord's Day theology

- your view of women preaching

- your expectations for pastoral support

Template:

`I believe corporate worship should be regulated by ... I believe preaching should function as ... I observe the Lord's Day as ...`

### 17. Christian Ethics

Use questions `Q97-Q104` to state:

- your approach to law and civil order

- war and peace

- marriage and divorce

- life ethics

- sexual ethics

- liberty and stewardship

Template:

`I believe Christian ethics under the new covenant should be governed by ... I believe the state may / may not ... I believe marriage is ... I believe human life from conception is ...`

### 18. Mission and Evangelism

Use questions `Q105-Q108` to state:

- what the church's mission is

- which evangelistic methods you endorse

- how you prioritize mission fields

- what role apologetics should play

Template:

`I believe the mission of the church is primarily ... I prefer evangelistic methods that ... I believe apologetics should ...`

### 19. Israel, Church, and Covenants

Use questions `Q109-Q114` to state:

- whether Israel and the church are one people or distinct peoples

- how land promises should be read

- who belongs to the new covenant

- how the Abrahamic and Mosaic covenants function now

Template:

`I believe the relationship between Israel and the church is ... I believe the land promises are ... I believe the new covenant includes ...`

### 20. Eschatology

Use questions `Q115-Q122` to state:

- your millennial position

- your tribulation and rapture view

- your doctrine of Israel's future

- your doctrine of hell

- your doctrine of the intermediate state

- your doctrine of the new creation

Template:

`I believe Christ will return ... in relation to the millennium. I believe the tribulation is ... I believe the destiny of the wicked is ... I believe the new creation is ...`

## How To Handle Mixed Or Hybrid Positions

You may find that your answers do not fit neatly into one named system. That is acceptable.

If your answers are mixed:

- say so explicitly

- identify the doctrinal loci where you differ from a standard system

- name the combination carefully

Example sentence:

`My theology is broadly Reformed in soteriology, Baptist in ecclesiology, continuationist in pneumatology, and premillennial in eschatology, though I do not hold every conclusion of each tradition.`

## How To Cite Scripture In The Final Document

Do not paste every proof text from the workbook into the final document.

Instead:

1. Use the strongest 2-6 texts for each paragraph.

2. Keep secondary or debated references in footnotes or an appendix if desired.

3. If a doctrine is highly disputed, include one short sentence acknowledging the alternative reading.

Example:

`I believe election is unconditional, grounded in God's gracious purpose rather than foreseen merit or faith (Ephesians 1:4-5; Romans 9:11-18; 2 Timothy 1:9).`

When a conclusion depends mainly on inference rather than one explicit proof text, say that honestly in your wording. That is usually a mark of rigor, not weakness.

## Optional Final Appendix

After your main document, you may add:

- a short glossary of technical terms

- a list of convictions held with lower confidence

- a section titled `Questions For Further Study`

- a one-page doctrinal summary for quick reference

## Final Quality Check

Before treating the document as finished, confirm:

- every major doctrinal locus is represented

- your strongest claims are supported by your strongest texts, not merely by the largest number of texts

- your key convictions are stated positively, not only as denials

- your wording is clear enough to be read by another Christian without explanation

- your cited verses actually support the sentence they follow

- your statements on debated doctrines fairly represent the main alternative position

- your final document sounds like a coherent theology, not a raw questionnaire export

The best final result is not the longest one. It is the clearest one: a document you can hand to another believer and say, accurately, this is what I believe.
